Macbeth

by William Shakespeare

Summary

Macbeth is a tragic play that tells the story of a Scottish general whose ambition leads him to treachery, murder, and madness. After receiving a prophecy from three witches, Macbeth murders King Duncan to take the throne. Wracked with guilt and paranoia, he becomes a tyrannical ruler until he is eventually overthrown.

Main Characters

  • Macbeth – A brave Scottish general who becomes consumed by ambition.
  • Lady Macbeth – Macbeth’s ambitious wife who encourages him to commit murder.
  • Banquo – Macbeth’s friend and fellow general, whose descendants the witches prophesy will rule.
  • King Duncan – The virtuous King of Scotland, murdered by Macbeth.
  • Macduff – A nobleman who opposes Macbeth and eventually kills him.
  • The Three Witches – Mysterious figures who prophesize Macbeth’s rise and fall.

Themes

  • Ambition – The destructive nature of unchecked ambition is central to the play.
  • Fate vs. Free Will – Macbeth struggles between accepting fate and making his own choices.
  • Guilt – Guilt haunts both Macbeth and Lady Macbeth after the murder.
  • Power and Corruption – The pursuit of power leads to tyranny and moral decay.

Famous Quotes
